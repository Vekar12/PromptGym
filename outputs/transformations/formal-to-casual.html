Formal → Casual Register Rewrite

Internal policy email rewritten for a startup Slack message

Formal — Original Email
Dear Team,

I am writing to inform you that, effective the commencement of the forthcoming quarter, the organisation shall be implementing a revised remote work policy. Employees are hereby required to be physically present at the office premises for a minimum of three days per calendar week. Exceptions may be granted at the sole discretion of the relevant department head, subject to submission of a formal request no fewer than five business days in advance. Failure to comply with the aforementioned stipulations may result in disciplinary proceedings being initiated in accordance with the Employee Conduct Framework.

Kindly acknowledge receipt of this communication.

Regards,
HR Department
Casual — Slack Rewrite
Hey everyone 👋

Quick update on our return-to-office policy starting next quarter:

In-office days: 3 days/week minimum.

Need flexibility? Just check with your manager at least 5 business days ahead — they can make exceptions when needed.

If you're not sure how this applies to your team, drop a message in #people-ops and we'll sort it out.

Thanks for being awesome 🙏
— People Ops
Key Changes Made
Formal phraseCasual versionWhy it works
"effective the commencement of the forthcoming quarter" → "starting next quarter" 6 words → 3. No meaning lost.
"Failure to comply may result in disciplinary proceedings" → Removed entirely Threat language kills psychological safety. Manager conversation handles edge cases.
"Kindly acknowledge receipt of this communication" → Removed (Slack's read receipts handle this) Channel-appropriate; don't replicate email formalities in chat.
"HR Department" → "People Ops" + emoji Team name matches company culture. Emoji adds warmth without being unprofessional.
